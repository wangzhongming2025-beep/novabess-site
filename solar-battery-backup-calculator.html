Backup sizing FAQ

How do I calculate battery size for backup power?

Add up the watts of the loads you want to keep on during an outage, multiply by the hours of autonomy you need, then divide by depth of discharge. A 1,000 W load for 8 hours needs 8 kWh usable; at 90% DoD that is about 8.9 kWh nameplate. Use the calculator above to turn this into a real model.

How many hours of backup will a 10 kWh battery give?

Divide usable kWh by your backed-up load in kW. A 10 kWh battery at 90% DoD gives ~9 kWh usable; running a 1,000 W (1 kW) essential load, that is about 9 hours per charge. Running a 3 kW load, it is about 3 hours. More load = fewer hours.

Can a solar battery backup recharge during a multi-day outage?

Yes, if you have rooftop PV. Sunlight recharges the bank each clear day, so a correctly sized system can ride out an open-ended outage — not just one charge. Enter your array size in the calculator to see daily recharge vs your daily draw.

Is LiFePO4 good for backup batteries?

Yes. LiFePO4 supports 80–100% depth of discharge (so a 10 kWh nameplate delivers close to 10 kWh usable) and lasts 5,000+ cycles, far more than lead-acid's ~500 cycles. That means a smaller, longer-lived bank for the same backup runtime.

Do I need solar to have a battery backup?

No. A battery backup also works charged from the grid (see our home battery backup without solar guide). But pairing it with rooftop PV lets it recharge for free during extended outages — the strongest setup for blackout-prone areas.
