Portable Power for Off-Grid & Construction Sites

PowerBoxOff-gridConstructionNo diesel

When there is no grid — or the grid is unreliable — a wheeled, plug-and-play battery delivers silent, fume-free power you roll to the job. NovaBESS PowerBox ships in 4 / 8 / 16 kWh blocks with AC + DC multi-port output, ready to run the moment it arrives.

Best fit: PowerBox is built for places a wall of batteries cannot go — construction sites, mines, telecom towers, field events and emergency response. It needs no grid tie-in and no diesel, runs at 4 / 8 / 16 kWh, and connects with standard AC and DC outlets so tools and equipment start immediately.

Why battery beats diesel on site

Diesel generators are the default on remote sites, but they carry hidden cost: fuel delivery, noise, fumes, maintenance and carbon compliance. A lithium portable power station removes all four. For a typical single-shift site running lights, drills, pumps and a site office, a 4–8 kWh unit covers core loads; add a second module for heavier or multi-shift work. Because PowerBox is wheeled and plug-and-play, one person moves and starts it — no crane, no electrician.

Where PowerBox earns its keep

  • Construction & renovation: power tools, site lighting and temporary office where the mains are not yet connected.
  • Mining & exploration: remote camps and instrumentation far from any line.
  • Telecom towers: backup for off-grid masts so a single outage does not drop a cell sector.
  • Emergency & disaster response: roll power to a command post, medical point or shelter within minutes.
  • Off-grid cabins & events: clean power for lighting, comms and small appliances.

Frequently asked questions

Can PowerBox run power tools on a construction site?

Yes. With AC + DC multi-port output it runs standard site tools, lighting and a temporary office. Choose 4 kWh for light single-shift use, 8–16 kWh for busier or multi-shift sites.

Does it need a grid connection to work?

No. PowerBox is off-grid by design — it delivers power wherever you roll it. You recharge it later from grid, solar or a generator.

How is it shipped internationally?

It is certified to UN38.3 (the UN transport test for lithium batteries), CE and IEC 62619, which streamlines air and sea freight versus uncertified units.
